### "Anniyan-style" motion-graphics experience — from scratch to deploy

Anything tagged **[BELIEF]** is a tradition/claim, not established fact — present it as such on the site. The "Anniyan website" look is reconstructed from descriptions of the 2005 film, not the actual (now-defunct) site, so treat it as *inspired-by*.

## 0. The Concept (the "Anniyan" hook)

In *Anniyan* (2005), a vigilante runs a dark, ritualistic website that catalogues wrongdoers and assigns them punishments from the **Garuda Puranam**. The visual feel: ominous, scripture-like, hooded/grim-reaper iconography, fire, Sanskrit/Tamil text, a sense of *divine judgement*.

We borrow the **mood and structure**, not the violence:
- Karuppu Swamy is literally the **God of Justice / dharma-enforcer / kaval deivam (guardian)** — a perfect thematic fit for a "judgement" aesthetic *without* the gore.
- The site becomes a **digital sanctum**: dark, fierce, reverent, cinematic. Each section "reveals" itself through motion graphics like a ritual unfolding.
- Reframe "punishment of sinners" → **"protection of the righteous & justice against evil."** Same intensity, devotional intent.

**One-line pitch:** *A cinematic digital shrine where the guardian deity Karuppu Swamy is revealed form by form, power by power, through scroll-triggered motion graphics.*

## 1. Karuppu Swamy — Master Content Reference

### 1.1 Core identity
- **Name:** Karuppu / Karuppasamy / Karuppannasamy / Karuppu Swamy. *Karuppu* = "black"; *Sami* = "god." → "The Black/Dark God."
- **Type:** Guardian deity (*kaval deivam*), village deity (*gramadevata*) and ancestral deity (*kuladevata*) in Dravidian folk religion.
- **Regions:** Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Sri Lanka; diaspora worldwide.
- **Role:** Fierce protector, upholder of justice, enforcer of dharma. One of the **21 folk deities associated with Ayyanar**.
- **Origin theory [VERIFY]:** Scholars (e.g. E. Kent) link his emergence to the **Nayaka period (mid-16th–18th c.)**, possibly modelled on *poligars* (local chieftains). Believed by devotees to have been a real historical protector who became deified.

### 1.2 Iconography (visual bible for the artwork)
| Element | Description | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Skin | Dark / black, muscular | Name origin; absorbs/drives out darkness |
| Face | Thick moustache, fierce wide eyes, fiery pink/red eyes | Vigilance, aggression |
| Hair | Long, tied in a **side-bun** | Nayaka-era martial style |
| Forehead | *Thiruneeru* (sacred ash) + kumkum | Sanctity |
| Weapon | **Aruval** (sword/sickle), sometimes bells attached; also bow/arrow or spear | Strength → justice |
| Adornment | Chains, rings, flower garlands | Power, devotion |
| Mount/companions [VERIFY — sources differ] | White horse; hunting dog (*vettai naai*); sometimes a lion | Martial guardian |
| Attendants | **7 Kannimar** (Saptha Kanni / seven virgins) | Divine retinue |
| Offerings shown | Lit cigar in mouth, alcohol, camphor | Folk (non-Vedic) worship |

### 1.3 The "Forms / Versions" of Karuppu — *for the "every version" requirement*
> **[BELIEF]** Tradition says there are **108 forms**. This number is devotional, not documented. Below are the *named, attested* variants — use these as your real catalogue and label the "108" as tradition.

- **Sangili Karuppan** — "chained Karuppu"; bound with chains, intense protective form (popular in Malaysia/Singapore).
- **Mangadu Karuppu**
- **Periya Karuppu** ("great/elder")
- **Chinna Karuppu** ("younger")
- **Sami Karuppu**
- **Sanganie / Sangani Baba** — Indo-Caribbean form (Trinidad, Guyana, Suriname, Guadeloupe, Martinique).
- **Dee Baba** — diaspora name.
- Worshipped alongside **Madurai Veeran** and **Muneeswarar**.
- **[BELIEF]** Some traditions identify him with **Kalki / Vishnu's hand (Kaiyannar)** or as embodying Kala Bhairava/Narasimha/Veerabhadra/Rudra. Present strictly as "according to some devotees."

> Site treatment: a **"Forms Gallery"** where each named form is a motion-graphic card that animates in. This directly serves your "every version of Karuppu" goal.

### 1.4 Powers & Abilities — *for the "his power and ability" requirement*
> **[BELIEF]** Folk-deity "powers" are devotional attributes, not a fixed canon. Frame as "devotees believe…"

1. **Guardianship** — shields villages, homes, communities from evil spirits and negative energy (historically stationed at the *kāṭu*/wilderness boundary of villages).
2. **Justice & retribution** — finds and punishes wrongdoers; senses deceit; "crooks cannot escape."
3. **Protection from black magic** — invoked to remove curses/evil eye.
4. **Oracle / divine messenger** — speaks through a chosen oracle in trance (*Swami Aadudhal*) to settle disputes.
5. **Boon-granting** — fulfils vows (*nேர்த்திக்கடன்*) when honoured.
6. **Strength & valour** — martial power, symbolised by the aruval.

### 1.5 Worship, rituals & festivals
- **Priesthood:** Non-Brahmin, local hereditary lineages; **non-Vedic / non-Agamic**.
- **Folk arts:** Villu Paattu, Karakattam, Koothu, oral ballads, folk theatre (his history is **oral**, not Sanskrit-textual).
- **Offerings:** Milk, ghee, camphor, cloves, alcohol, cigars; animal sacrifice (goat/pig/chicken) in some traditions. *Handle this respectfully and factually.*
- **Ceremonies [VERIFY spellings]:** Kodei Vila, Mayaana Vettei, Kalari Thiruvila, Theemithi (fire-walking), Nadu Kal Valipadu, Aruval Vazhipadu.
- **Annual festival:** Flag hoisting opens it; typically **3 days**; villagers stay within the village; oracle enters trance.

### 1.6 Major temples
- **Azhagar Koil, near Madurai** — most important / considered original; other temples take soil from here. **[VERIFY]**
- Mettukulam (near Coimbatore) — dedicated solely to Karuppu.
- Tiruverkadu (near Chennai) — with Bhadrakali & Shiva.
- Thirumalaiyam Palayam (near Salem).
- **Sabarimala [VERIFY]** — Karuppu cited as a guardian of the premises (claim appears in popular sources; confirm).

### 1.7 Global diaspora
Worshipped in Malaysia, Singapore, Sri Lanka, South Africa, Mauritius, Réunion, Fiji, Seychelles, and the Indo-Caribbean (Trinidad & Tobago, Guyana, Suriname, Guadeloupe, Martinique) under names like Sangili Karuppan, Sangani/Sanganie Baba, Dee Baba.

### 4.2 Typography
- **Display (English):** a bold cinematic serif/blackletter-adjacent face (e.g. *Cinzel*, *Cormorant*) — evokes scripture. **[VERIFY licensing]**
- **Tamil:** *Catamaran*, *Mukta Malar*, or *Hind Madurai* (Google Fonts, Tamil-supporting). Always pair Tamil + English for names.
- **Body:** clean sans (Inter / Noto Sans / Noto Sans Tamil).
- Rule: never let decorative fonts harm Tamil legibility.

### 4.3 Motion language
- Reveal-on-scroll, ember particles, fire flicker, slow zoom (Ken Burns) on stills, slash/wipe transitions, parallax depth.
- **Restraint:** keep one "hero" motion moment per page; subtle everywhere else. Avoid the cluttered, dated feel the original Anniyan site was criticised for.

## 5. Motion Graphics with Google "Flow" — Production Plan

> **Honesty flag:** "Google Flow" most likely means **Flow**, Google's AI filmmaking tool built on **Veo** (launched 2025). It generates **video clips**, not code-embeddable web animations. So the pipeline is *generate clip → export video → embed*. Confirm current export formats/licensing before relying on it. Flow is **not** Lottie/Framer Motion.

### 5.1 Shot list (generate each as a clip)
| ID | Scene | Where used | ~Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| MG-01 | Embers + aruval slash + Tamil name forms in fire | Home hero (loop) | 8–12s |
| MG-02 | Deity silhouette turning, eyes ignite | /guardian | 5s |
| MG-03 | Chains wrapping (Sangili Karuppan) | /forms feature | 4s |
| MG-04 | Six power sigils igniting one by one | /powers | 6s |
| MG-05 | Fire-walking / Theemithi ambience | /festivals | 6s |
| MG-06 | Map ignition — nodes lighting globally | /diaspora | 5s |

### 5.2 Prompt template for Flow/Veo
> *"Cinematic, dark devotional scene. [SUBJECT]. Color grade: near-black background, deep reds, ember-orange glow, gold highlights. Slow, reverent camera. Volumetric smoke, floating ash particles, firelight. Photoreal but mythic. No text overlay. 16:9, loopable."*

Fill `[SUBJECT]` per shot. Generate, export, then optimise (see §6).

### 5.3 Web optimisation of clips
- Export → compress to **MP4 (H.264)** + **WebM (VP9/AV1)** for fallback.
- Hero loops: muted, autoplay, `playsinline`, poster image, lazy-load below fold.
- Provide a **reduced-motion** static fallback (`prefers-reduced-motion`).
- Code-level motion (scroll triggers, particles, transitions) handled by **GSAP + Lenis** and/or **Lottie** — *not* Flow.

## 6. Technical Build (recommended stack)

### 6.1 Stack
- **Framework:** Next.js (App Router) + TypeScript — SSR/SEO + dynamic content.
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS + CSS variables from §4.1.
- **Motion:** GSAP (ScrollTrigger), Lenis (smooth scroll), Framer Motion (component transitions), Lottie (vector micro-anims).
- **Maps:** Mapbox GL or Leaflet (temples + diaspora).
- **CMS (the "dynamic" part):** Sanity / Strapi / Payload — so you can add forms, temples, festivals without redeploying.
- **Media:** Cloudinary or Mux for video hosting/transcoding (don't self-host big MP4s).
- **Deploy:** Vercel (pairs natively with Next.js). Cloudflare for CDN/caching.

### 6.2 Why "dynamic"
Content (Forms, Powers, Temples, Festivals, Gallery) lives in the CMS as collections. The site fetches and renders them → you update content in a dashboard, the motion-graphic templates stay the same.

## 8. Performance, Accessibility, Ethics

- **Performance:** lazy-load video, AV1/WebM, image `next/image`, code-split heavy maps. Target LCP < 2.5s.
- **Accessibility:** `prefers-reduced-motion` static fallbacks; captions/alt for clips; AA contrast (the dark palette needs care on the red text — test it); keyboard nav.
- **Cultural respect:** This is a living faith. Keep tone reverent and factual; clearly separate **scholarship** from **devotional belief**; handle animal-sacrifice and alcohol-offering facts neutrally, not sensationally. Credit oral-tradition sources.
- **Don't replicate the violent framing of the film** — borrow only the cinematic gravitas.
